# Story DNA Template

## Title

Example: The House on Banana Island

## Logline

Example: A broke but brilliant young woman becomes a live-in assistant for a wealthy Lagos family, only to discover that their luxury life is built on a secret that could destroy everyone.

## Genre

Example: Family drama, mystery, romance, social class drama

## Target Audience

Example: Nigerian and African diaspora viewers who enjoy emotional family secrets, rich-poor tension, romance, betrayal, and cliffhanger episodes.

## Core Conflict

Example: Amara needs the job to save her mother, but every day inside the mansion brings her closer to exposing the family that pays her.

## Emotional Engine

Example: The story runs on shame, ambition, loyalty, class pressure, forbidden attraction, and the pain of being treated as invisible.

## Main Character

Example: Amara Okeke, 24, a smart and proud woman from Ajegunle who hides her fear behind sharp humor and stubborn confidence.

## Main Desire

Example: Amara wants enough money to pay her mother's hospital bills and prove that poverty did not define her future.

## Main Fear

Example: She fears becoming powerless like her father, who died after being betrayed by people he trusted.

## Secret or Hidden Pressure

Example: Amara does not know that her father's death is connected to the family she now works for.

## Series Potential

Example: Each episode can reveal a new secret inside the mansion while Amara becomes emotionally tangled with the family's rebellious son, Tobi.

## Notes

Example: Keep the tone emotional and high-stakes. The story should feel glamorous on the surface but painful underneath.
